# Summary

The BISD Framework is an open bilingual STEM instructional framework designed for K-5 Two-Way Immersion (TWI) programs. The framework integrates scientific inquiry, collaborative digital learning, experiential science, and bilingual academic language development aligned with NGSS Science and Engineering Practices. BISD was developed through practitioner-researcher action research conducted in a rural North Carolina bilingual education setting serving English Language Learners.

# Statement of Need

Despite the rapid expansion of Two-Way Immersion programs across the United States, bilingual STEM instruction at the K-5 level remains structurally underdeveloped. Existing science resources for bilingual classrooms are frequently translated from English-language curricula rather than designed specifically for bilingual learning environments. This creates instructional gaps in scientific inquiry, academic Spanish development, collaborative STEM learning, and equitable access to NGSS-aligned instruction.

The BISD Framework was developed in response to four recurring deficits identified through practitioner research and educator surveys conducted across bilingual instructional settings:

1. Lack of structured Spanish-first scientific inquiry systems.
2. Limited access to laboratory-based STEM resources in under-resourced schools.
3. Technology integration models centered on individual rather than collaborative learning.
4. Absence of transferable bilingual STEM curriculum guides for TWI educators.

The framework addresses these gaps through an integrated and transferable instructional system designed specifically for bilingual K-5 classrooms.

# Framework Overview

The BISD Framework consists of five integrated components.

## IBICI

IBICI (Observe, Question, Investigate, Analyze, Communicate) is a bilingual scientific inquiry cycle aligned with NGSS Science and Engineering Practices. The framework incorporates structured academic language scaffolds that support English Language Learners in developing evidence-based scientific communication in Spanish.

## MACE

MACE provides experiential STEM investigation protocols using low-cost and community-based resources. The model supports scientific investigation in environments without laboratory infrastructure through engineering design, digital investigation, and community-centered observation.

## TIDE

TIDE establishes collaborative digital learning structures that transform technology use into team-based scientific inquiry. Students rotate through differentiated scientific roles including investigator, data collector, engineer, and communicator while participating in structured peer review and collaborative discussion protocols.

## BISD Guide

The BISD Guide provides a transferable K-5 implementation structure aligned with NGSS, ACCESS for ELLs, and bilingual instructional practices. Resources include lesson templates, science journals, assessment tools, and implementation supports for educators working across varying language proficiency levels.

## FRAME

FRAME functions as the dissemination and scalability component of the BISD Framework. The model was designed for adaptation across bilingual STEM programs seeking reproducible instructional structures for equitable STEM participation.

# Preliminary Implementation

Initial implementation of the BISD Framework began during the 2024-2025 academic year in a fifth-grade bilingual classroom setting. Preliminary classroom observations documented high levels of collaborative engagement, increased use of academic Spanish during scientific discussions, and growth in evidence-based science writing.

Follow-up educator surveys indicated that teachers adopting components of the framework reported improved organization of scientific discourse and reduced preparation time through the use of transferable instructional templates and inquiry structures.

These preliminary findings suggest that the BISD Framework may provide a feasible and scalable approach for bilingual STEM instruction in resource-variable K-5 educational settings.
